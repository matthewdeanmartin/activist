# Exclude or Flag

Instances where the policy text creates a real conflict with running this
bot, sorted by severity. This feeds the `[identity].instances` allowlist in
`activist.toml` (`real_overview.md` §4) — treat this file as the source of
truth for *why* a domain is absent from that list, so it doesn't get
re-added accidentally by `discover.py` (`spec/improve_policy_downloads.md`
§2) without a second look.

## Flag — thematic/values conflict, judgment call

| Instance | Conflict | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| `climatejustice.social` | Thematically a *perfect* fit (climate justice, individuals/small-orgs welcome) but explicitly: "We don't post anything generated by AI except to enhance accessibility," and lists AI alongside nuclear/crypto as "harmful technologies" they don't promote. | This is the sharpest tension in the whole corpus — the best-fit *audience* is the most AI-skeptical *policy*. Two options: (a) exclude, respecting the letter of "we don't post AI content" even though the bot isn't *the instance* posting, just an account on it; (b) the account's own AI-disclosure (per `02_attribution_and_ai_content.md` §1) makes the bot's nature unambiguous, and let a human decide per-post whether this instance's audience is the right call. **Recommendation: exclude for now** — the instance's own framing suggests they'd consider an AI-driven account posting here to be against the spirit of the rule even if not the letter, and reputational risk to an advocacy bot from being called out on its "home turf" instance is disproportionate to the upside. |
| `metalhead.club` | "No upload of AI generated art." | Not a conflict for text-only posts (current scope). Re-flag only if/when image attachments are added — note this in `poster_service.md`'s "explicitly out of scope" list if that work ever starts. |

## Explicitly NOT flagged (checked and cleared)

To avoid `discover.py` or a future reviewer re-litigating these, a note on
categories that *sound* relevant but aren't, for this bot:

- **"No political campaigning / electoral propaganda" rules** (seen on
  several instances, e.g. "no electoral propaganda/campaigning," "no
  propaganda from politicians or political parties that seek to win
  elections"). These target *party-affiliated / electoral* accounts. A
  green-energy advocacy bot discussing technology and policy is not a
  political party or campaign account — this category doesn't apply unless
  the persona starts endorsing candidates or parties, which is out of scope.
- **"No accounts representing political parties / brands" rules** (e.g.
  `theblower.au`'s brand exclusion, `climatejustice.social`'s "individuals...
  belong here, represent groups → climatejustice.global instead"). The bot
  is run as an individual/persona account, not a registered org — clear.
- **General "no spam/no excessive promotion"** — covered as an ongoing
  content-shape requirement in `02_attribution_and_ai_content.md` §4, not
  an instance-exclusion issue.

## How this list should be maintained

When `discover.py` adds new candidate domains to `servers.txt`
(`spec/improve_policy_downloads.md` §2), and `policy_fetcher` produces a new
`policies/<domain>.txt`, re-run the greps this distillation was built from
before adding the domain to any identity's `instances` list:

```bash
grep -i -E "no ai|llm agent|generative ai|bot account|automated" policies/<domain>.txt
```

If a new instance trips one of these patterns, add it to the appropriate
table above rather than re-deriving the whole distillation.
