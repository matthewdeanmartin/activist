# Attribution, AI Disclosure & Misinformation

This is the bucket most relevant to a pipeline that **transforms** content
(news articles → toots, replies to mentions) using an LLM. Two distinct
rule families show up across the corpus, plus a third that's really about
the *source material* rather than the bot itself.

## 1. "Content created by others must be attributed, and use of generative
AI must be disclosed"

Verbatim or near-verbatim on **4 instances**: `mastodon.social`,
`mastodon.online`, `mstdn.social`, `masto.nu` (this last one adds: "Profiles
that only post AI-generated content will not be tolerated"). These are all
big general-purpose instances — likely targets for this bot.

This is two separate obligations:

- **Attribution** — when the post is *about* or *derived from* a specific
  article/source (the fetcher's `source_url`/`source_title` fields,
  `real_overview.md` §3 schema), the published toot must credit it. This is
  naturally satisfied if the bot's output format always includes a link
  back to the source — which it should anyway for "advocacy" content
  (gives readers somewhere to go).
- **AI disclosure** — the *account* (not necessarily every post) must
  disclose that an LLM is involved in producing content. The bot-flag +
  bio approach in `01_bot_disclosure.md` substantially covers this if the
  bio says something like "AI-assisted posts, human-reviewed before
  publishing." A per-post disclosure (footer tag, hashtag, or CW) is the
  stronger reading and matches the `poster_service.md` P2 "first-flight
  checklist" item: *"verify the disclosure footer renders."*

**Recommendation:** bake a short, fixed disclosure string into the post
template (e.g. a trailing `(AI-assisted, human-reviewed)` or a profile-wide
statement plus a consistent hashtag like `#AIAssisted`). Make it part of
the template, not something the LLM decides to include — a static string
is mechanically guaranteed to be present, an LLM-authored disclosure is not.

## 2. "No AI-generated content" (stricter, smaller set)

| Instance | Rule | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| `mastodon.au` | "No AI Accounts, or AI generated content, without express prior approval" | Pre-approval gate, like `hachyderm.io`'s bot invite — not satisfiable by pipeline code. Skip unless approval obtained. |
| `metalhead.club` | "No upload of AI generated art" | Only applies if the bot ever attaches images; text-only posts are unaffected. Note for future media-attachment work (currently out of scope per `poster_service.md` "Explicitly out of scope"). |
| `climatejustice.social` | "We do not promote harmful technologies like nuclear power, AI or crypto currencies. We don't post anything generated by AI except to enhance accessibility." | Direct content-topic conflict — see `05_exclude_or_flag.md`. Ironic given the instance's exact thematic fit (climate justice), but the AI-skepticism is explicit and the bot's entire output is AI-generated text. |
| `nerdculture.de` | "No AI (LLM) Agents (no exceptions)" | Already covered in `01_bot_disclosure.md` — full exclusion. |

## 3. Misinformation / "don't share false information" — the source-material gate

Some variant of "no false/misleading information," "no disinformation," or
"no conspiracy theories" appears on **the large majority** of instances
(it's effectively a fediverse-wide norm, not an outlier rule). For a green-
energy advocacy bot the practical risk isn't *malice* — it's an LLM
**hallucinating a stat, a study, or a citation** when summarizing/rewriting
a fetched article.

This maps directly onto the existing **unverified-link rule** noted in
`real_overview.md` §2 and the `spec/improve_policy_downloads.md` §3.2 LLM
guard: *"never trust a URL the LLM invents that wasn't in the input."* The
same discipline should extend to **factual claims**, not just URLs:

- Numbers, percentages, dates, named studies, or quotes that appear in the
  LLM's output but **not** in the source article text should be flagged for
  human review (or stripped) before the post reaches `pending_review`.
- A small set of instances call out **public-health/political
  misinformation** specifically as a harder line (`pravda.me`-style
  instances, `mastodon.bot`'s "Disinformation" rule, etc.) — green energy
  content can brush against climate-science and public-health framing
  (e.g. comparing emissions to health outcomes), so this isn't purely
  hypothetical for this bot's topic area.

This is the strongest case in the whole corpus for **an LLM-based
moderation pass** (per `real_overview.md` §2, "LLM moderation layered on
top of MockModerator") rather than pure mechanical checking — "is this
claim supported by the source text" is a semantic question. But it's a
*pre-human-review* flag, not a publish/reject decision — the human in
`pending_review` is still the actual gate (see `04_checks_by_enforcement.md`).

## 4. Topical "stay on advocacy, not spam/promotion"

Several instances draw a line between *advocacy/discussion* and
*advertising/promotion* (`mastodon.bot`: "Spam: Commercial advertising is
banned... excessive promotion is banned"; `mindly.social`: accounts that
"only post links to external news sources without providing any commentary
or attempting to start a discussion will be limited or suspended").

For this bot, the practical takeaway: **the transform step must add
commentary/framing, not just relay a headline + link**. This is presumably
already the design (it's an "opinion"-bearing persona per
`real_overview.md` §2 "persona/ opinions"), but it's worth treating as a
hard requirement of the output format — a post that is *only* "Title. Link."
is both a policy risk (looks like link-spam/bot-crossposting, the thing
`ohai.social`/`mindly.social`/`c.im` single out) and presumably off-brand
for an "activist" voice anyway.
